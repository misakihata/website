I read Constance Classen and David Howes' chapter "The museum as sensescapes: Western sensibilities and Indigenous artefacts" from the book "Sensible Objects" as part of my core reading for my masters studies. This was the first time I came across the concept of occularcentrism. Some of the themes mentioned in this article include the importance of touch in 17th and 18th century European collections compared to the dominance of sight in the modern museum; the Western association of the “lower” races with the “lower” senses; the links between museum display and imperialism; and, the complex sensory lives of indigenous artifacts in their cultures of origin.
