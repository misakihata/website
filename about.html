About me

Brief history

Right now, I work at NHS digital as an User centred design graduate. I did my first placement in the NHS Service Manual team as a user researcher, followed by a second placement working as a user researcher/service designer in 4 different coronavirus projects for the NHS Test and Trace service.

Before working in design, I worked for 2 years as a TV news producer reporting on world affairs for TV Asahi, a Japanese national broadcaster. And prior to that, I was studying Anthropology in various places including Oxford, Tokyo, Copenhagen and Berlin.


Skills

I am a visual thinker, I draw illustrations and graphs to help process, analyse and communicate things. I enjoy facilitating workshops, telling and listening to stories and making digital, hand-drawn and knitted prototypes.
My background in anthropology and journalism means I highly respect thorough research and analysis, and those two things are vital in my design process.

I speak English and Japanese, and am currently learning Swedish.
